Flying: My Journey to Cloud Computing

Greetings AWS! I am Ikram, a Theology PhD candidate at the University of Oxford. I am an applicant for the Associate Solutions Architect Internship position at AWS. I decided to write this piece to document my motivations for this change in career direction.

For as long as I can remember, I wanted to become a professor. I wanted to spend my life researching and teaching a subject I loved. I did my undergraduate at the LSE, a subsequent masters at Birmingham, leading me to a fully-funded scholarship to study at Oxford.

Since joining Oxford, I have attended academic conferences, wrote papers for journal submission, refined my presentation technique and received training as a graduate teaching assistant. However, my career plans changed when I became a father!

<<------ That's Asiyah, my daughter. Asiyah was born in July 2021 and her birth sparked a re-prioritisation of my life and ambitions. Becoming an early-stage academic would entail precarious contractual conditions, and I wanted more for my family. I conducted a SWOT analysis and researched careers that aligned with my wants. I wanted an intellectually challenging career that is client-facing and involves working closely in teams.

I pursued commercial law for a few months, aggressively researching firms, attending law fairs, and networking with associates at elite city law firms. I landed two sought-after vacation schemes for the summer. However, the work-life balance commercial law offers (i.e., 10-12 hour working days as standard) never settled correctly in my heart. After all, I made this dramatic change of heart for my daughter. What use is a stable career for my daughter if I don't see my daughter!

In searching for other options, I came across the role of a cloud solutions architect and cloud computing more generally, and I immediately fell in love. As a specialist in Islamic law and traditional studies, the possibilities of cloud solutions in aiding my research interests were immense. Specifically, utilising cloud infrastructure, machine learning and artificial intelligence to make accessible centuries of scholarship through manuscript verification, the historiography of foundational sources such as the Quran and Hadith and online repositories of Arabic books. Learning about these possibilities and building a career in this industry offered a genuine intellectual interest. The industry provides this alignment, calls upon intellectual challenges and problem solving, and more: work-life harmony!

In late December 2021, I decided to start preparing for a career as a solutions architect. I have begun learning: the Linux OS and CLI, SQL, and Python. I have also started preparing for industry recognised certifications. Currently, I am revising for the AWS CCP exam to sit it in March 2022. I aspire to pass the AWS SAA certification by July 2022 and the Cisco 200-301 exam in August/September 2022. Despite having a PhD thesis to write, I am making these bold predictions because I am determined to obtain a graduate scheme as an associate solutions architect.
